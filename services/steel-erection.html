Steel Erection & Installation

Professional structural steel erection and installation services for commercial and industrial construction projects. From steel beam placement to complete structural framing — Highland Welding has been erecting steel across the Hudson Valley since 1946.

What Is Steel Erection?

Steel erection is the process of assembling and installing fabricated structural steel components on a construction site. It involves setting columns, connecting beams, placing bar joists, and securing every connection — transforming raw fabricated steel into the standing framework of a building.

It's one of the most critical phases of any commercial or industrial construction project. Steel erection requires experienced ironworkers, precise crane operation, certified welders, and rigorous attention to safety and alignment. At Highland Welding, we've been erecting steel throughout the Hudson Valley since 1946.

Steel Erection Capabilities

  • Steel beam and column installation
  • Structural steel framing
  • Bar joist and deck installation
  • Connection bolting and welding
  • Steel stair and platform erection
  • Mezzanine installation
  • Steel canopy and awning structures
  • Miscellaneous metals installation

Our Process

Every steel erection project starts with close coordination with the general contractor. We review erection drawings, confirm delivery sequences, and plan crane placement to ensure safe and efficient steel setting from day one.

Steel is delivered directly from our Peekskill fabrication shop — cut, drilled, and fit-up ready. On site, our crew handles crane signaling, steel placement, plumbing and aligning columns, and making permanent connections. Our certified welders complete field welds on-site as required, and all work is subject to quality inspections throughout the erection process.

Why Choose Highland Welding for Steel Erection?

When you work with Highland Welding, you get a single source for both fabrication and erection. We fabricate the steel in our shop and erect it on your job site — eliminating coordination headaches between separate fabricators and erectors.
