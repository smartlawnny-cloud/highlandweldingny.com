Custom Metal Fabrication

If you can sketch it, describe it, or bring in a sample — we can build it. Custom metal fabrication for homeowners, contractors, and businesses across Westchester and the Hudson Valley.

What Is Custom Metal Fabrication?

Custom fabrication is metalwork built specifically for your application — not off a shelf. It could be a one-of-a-kind decorative gate, a replacement component for a machine that hasn't been manufactured in 30 years, a retrofit system for a delivery vehicle, or structural elements for a unique building design. Whatever it is, if it involves steel or metal, we can fabricate it.

What We Fabricate

  • Decorative ironwork — gates, railings, window guards, balconies
  • Industrial components — brackets, frames, mounts, enclosures
  • Retrofit systems and custom equipment attachments
  • Replacement parts for discontinued or unavailable components
  • Structural components for construction and renovation
  • Custom trailers, frames, and hauling equipment
  • Architectural metalwork for commercial interiors and exteriors
  • Production runs for repeat components

Our Process

Bring us your sketch, blueprint, sample, or just a description of what you need. We'll assess the job, discuss material options (carbon steel, stainless, aluminum), provide a quote, and get it built. Most custom jobs start with a phone call or a quick visit to the shop.

  • Consultation — describe or show us what you need
  • Material selection — we guide you on the right metal for the application
  • Fabrication — built in our Peekskill shop with quality control throughout
  • Delivery or pickup — your choice

Why Custom vs. Off-the-Shelf?

Sometimes the right part simply doesn't exist as a manufactured product. Other times, a custom solution is stronger, better fitting, or longer-lasting than anything available to buy. Our customers have brought us problems nobody else could solve — and we've solved them.

Volume & Timeline

We handle both one-off prototypes and production runs of repeat components. Timeline depends on complexity and material availability — most custom jobs are quoted with a realistic delivery date upfront. We don't overpromise.
